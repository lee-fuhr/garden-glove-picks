A good pair of thorn-proof gloves is the difference between a pleasant afternoon in the rose bed and a session of curse‑word‑filled bandaging. Budget options don’t have to mean shredded palms; the trick is looking for dense, multi‑layer materials (leather or synthetic blends) and a snug fit that keeps thorns from punching through at the tips. You also want cuffs that cover your wrists – that’s where most sneaky brambles strike. In this round‑up, we’ve focused on gloves that cost roughly a few dozen dollars or less, based on widespread owner reports and expert recommendations. All three models have proven themselves against rose bushes, blackberries, and barberry. We’ve ranked them by how well they protect while still letting you tie a knot or pull a weed.
